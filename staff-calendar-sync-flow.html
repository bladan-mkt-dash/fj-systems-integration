Appointment Sync — Calendars, Record Flags & BigQuery History

Near-term (in scope): appointments are scheduled in AthenaNet (clinical) or Zenoti (wellness), then reflected outbound — BigQuery always; staff GHL calendars when the provider is a linked GHL user; patient GHL/Zenoti record flags (not patient calendars). Not in scope yet: patients booking in GHL and writing back to AthenaNet (possible later; see callout — depends on Google Meet / telehealth HIPAA posture).

Example: clinical appt in AthenaNet → GHL contact flagged + Zenoti guest profile flagged (when linked). Wellness appt in Zenoti → GHL contact flagged only. BigQuery always stores the appointment (including cancels/reschedules) for cross-system history and the Admin Check List.

Three outcomes, one webhook — BigQuery is non-optional Track A and Track B improve day-to-day staff UX. The warehouse appointment fact is the integration contract for reporting: without it, appointment date-range filters and “Upcoming Appointment Issues” cannot be guaranteed.

Every linked patient profile should show that an appointment occurred (or was cancelled) and retain that history for marketing, front desk, and reporting — without re-booking in the destination system and without overwriting AthenaNet clinical data once populated.

Direction today: AthenaNet / Zenoti → hub → GHL (+ BigQuery) — one-way.
Always: BigQuery appointment fact (status-aware).
Track A (staff): busy block on that provider’s GHL user calendar when linked.
Track B (patient): record flag on GHL contact and/or Zenoti guest — not a patient calendar; skip the system where the appt was booked.

Calendar events should use busy blocks or de-identified labels unless GHL is confirmed HIPAA-aligned for full clinical detail on staff calendars. Gap detection and provider action lists are defined in the Operations reporting layer — this map supplies the appointment feed.

Future (optional two-way): Patient self-schedules in GHL → hub creates / updates the AthenaNet appointment. Architecturally feasible as a reverse webhook path, but out of scope until product and compliance decide that Google Meet (or the telehealth surface used with GHL) can operate under an acceptable HIPAA / BAA posture. Until then, keep AthenaNet as the system of record for clinical booking; GHL only reflects staff calendars and patient flags.
